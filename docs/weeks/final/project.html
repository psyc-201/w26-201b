Final Project

Overview

The final project is your opportunity to apply the statistical modeling skills you’ve learned throughout the course to a research question of your choice.

Timeline

Date Milestone
Week 8 (Feb 27) Project proposal due
Week 10 (Mar 13) Draft analysis due (optional feedback)
Final Exam Week (Mar 21) Final project due

Requirements

1. Research Question

Formulate a clear, answerable research question that requires statistical analysis.

2. Data

  • Use a publicly available dataset OR
  • Use data from your own research (with appropriate permissions)
  • Minimum 100 observations recommended

3. Analysis

Your analysis should include:

  1. Exploratory Data Analysis
    • Summary statistics
    • Visualizations
    • Data quality assessment
  2. Statistical Modeling
    • At least one model from the course (GLM, LMM, etc.)
    • Model diagnostics
    • Model comparison (if appropriate)
  3. Inference or Prediction
    • Parameter estimates with uncertainty
    • Hypothesis tests or confidence intervals
    • OR cross-validated prediction performance
  4. Interpretation
    • What do the results mean?
    • Limitations
    • Future directions

4. Deliverables

  1. Rendered .qmd file (HTML or PDF)
    • Introduction and research question
    • Methods
    • Results (with code)
    • Discussion
  2. Data and code
    • All code should be reproducible
    • Include data or instructions for obtaining it

Grading Rubric

Component Points
Research question clarity 10
Data preparation and EDA 15
Appropriate model choice 20
Model fitting and diagnostics 20
Interpretation and conclusions 20
Code quality and reproducibility 10
Writing and presentation 5
Total 100

Proposal Template

Submit a 1-page proposal including:

  1. Research question (1-2 sentences)
  2. Data source (what data, where from, sample size)
  3. Proposed analysis (what models/methods)
  4. Expected challenges (what might be tricky?)

Example Projects

Past successful projects have included:

  • Predicting movie ratings with mixed models (repeated measures per user)
  • Analyzing experimental psychology data with factorial ANOVA/LMM
  • Logistic regression for predicting customer churn
  • Power analysis for a planned study
  • Factor analysis of survey responses
